Project Description

It is well-known that cancer metastasis is the primary cause behind many cancer patients’ death. Tumor cells first intrude the tissues surrounding the primary cancer cells, then transfer or exudate to other distal tissues with the help of Circulating Tumor Cells (CTCs) through blood circulation or lymphatic networks. The transfer and exudation, are exactly the main reasons behind cancerous lesions. Obviously, research on the capture, detection and elimination of CTCs plays a vital role in combating cancer. However, catching CTCs has never been easy. On the one hand, the cells’ scarce amount in a person’s body and its tiny size make it hard for us to detect them. On the other, the existing CTCs capture devices usually damage the cells, causing the loss of their integrity and activity, which sets barriers for future research. Therefore, it is a challenge to capture circulating tumor cells while keeping them intact and active. In order to do this, we decided to design a prison garb for CTCs to keep them in captivity and under protection. Our prison garb has the following characteristics:

Aptamer——the hunter of our prison garb

The aptamers serve as targets for specific priming capture in our prison garb. Their o oligonucleotide sequences are substitutions for antibodies in therapeutic and diagnostic applications. The aptamers are stable under various circumstances and can be synthesized and highly purified in laboratories easily. Surprisingly, modifying aptamers seems not to affect their affinity to the target, thus we can decorate our prison garb with aptamers, the hunters, for the detection and capture of CTCs. We attempt to develop a mechanism which uses the binding of adapters with CTCs as a trigger, so that we can accurately control our capture.

Quantum dots ——the alertor of our prison garb

Quantum dots (QDs), a kind of nanocrystals with core-shell structure which are attached to the garb, help us catch CTCs visually. Compared with conventional fluorescent dyes, they have narrow excitation and wide emission spectra. Meanwhile, the photostability and quantum yield of QDs are better than those of fluorescent dyes. So QDs have been applied more and more frequently in bioimaging and analysis. By combining magnetic quantum dots into the capture system, we realize directional lighting of captured CTC.

DNA hydrogel ——the fetter of our prison grab

The hydrogel system presents how we guarantee the controllability of our prison garb. We want to make DNA hydrogel form around CTCs through specific structure DNA cross-linking so as to achieve the coating and protection of the CTCs. Taking it into consideration, the designed DNA hydrogels should be equipped with the following functions. Firstly, start cross-linking according to the signal of specific aptamers of CTCs to ensure the correctness of the captured cells. Secondly, be convenient for us to fill quantum dots into it in order to recognize fluorescent labeling and magnetic adsorption-based capture. Thirdly, it can be coated or released in a mild way to ensure the physiological activity of the coated CTCs.
